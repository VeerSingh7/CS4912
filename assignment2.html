2. Technical Analysis & Cost Comparison

Why the Random Total Cost is Lower:

The total cost metric is artificially lowered for random pairs because it scales strictly with the total count of matches. Consecutive frames share substantial localized geometric contexts yielding an average of ~3,891 matches vs ~456 matches for random frames.

However, looking at the Per-Match Distance tells the objective truth: The consecutive array (139.1 avg cost) is comprised of higher quality matched invariants compared to the random array (155.7 avg cost). Only the strongest, most global keypoints bridge large view discrepancies, resulting in a low absolute total matched quantity but much lower fidelity.
