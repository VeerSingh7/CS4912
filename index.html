Seeing the World through Geometry

A collection of photography experiments demonstrating core Computer Vision principles: Perspective, Projection, and Depth.

01. Perspective Distortion

Analyzing how focal length affects facial feature proportions. The closer the camera, the more exaggerated the features appear due to perspective projection.

Close Range (Wide Angle)

Subject captured from ~1ft away. Note the "bulbous" nose effect and receding ears relative to the face.

Far Range (Telephoto)

Subject captured from ~10ft away. Features appear flattened and more proportionate to reality.

02. Scene Compression

Demonstrating Z-axis compression. Long focal lengths compress the perceived distance between foreground and background objects.

Telephoto Compression

High zoom from a distance. The background buildings appear looming and close to the foreground subjects.

Wide Angle Expansion

Standard view from closer range. The distance between objects is exaggerated, showing true depth.

03. Projective vs Orthographic

Parallel lines converge to vanishing points in perspective projection, but remain parallel in orthographic (or pseudo-orthographic) views.

Perspective Projection

Standard shot. Red lines indicate convergence towards vanishing points.

Pseudo-Orthographic

Telephoto shot from distance. Lines appear nearly parallel, mimicking an isometric view.
